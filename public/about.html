THE ROAD I TRAVELED

I finished high school in 2019 and began studying medicine at the Myanmar National University of Community Health. After completing the first semester exams, the COVID-19 pandemic began, and I returned home. Just a month later, the military coup occurred, and I chose to stop studying at the university, as I did not want to be part of an institution under the military regime. I spent nearly a year helping my family with farming, but I eventually realized it wasn’t the right path for me. In 2022, I began studying Japanese (N5 level) with the goal of entering a university in Japan. In 2023, I shifted my focus to IT and started studying NCC-National Computing Centre (UK-accredited by Ofqual) at New Next International College. In 2023, I also studied Digital Marketing and applied my knowledge practically by writing content and creating video scripts with my team for the New Next International College social media page. As the situation in Myanmar grew more dangerous, I decided to move to Japan for safety and education. I arrived in Osaka in May 2025, and I’m currently studying Japanese at the N3 level at Osaka Christian College while looking for job opportunities.
